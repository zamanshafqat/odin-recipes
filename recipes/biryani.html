Nehari

Description

Nehari is a rich, aromatic Pakistani dish known for its deep flavors and slow-cooked tenderness. It is typically made with slow-cooked meat, a blend of spices, and a thick gravy, best enjoyed with naan or paratha.

Ingredients

  • Meat (beef shank, lamb, or mutton) – 1 kg
  • Onions – 2 large, sliced
  • Ghee or oil – ½ cup
  • Ginger and garlic paste – 1 tbsp each
  • Flour (for thickening) – 2 tbsp
  • Water – 6 to 8 cups
  • Red chili, turmeric, coriander powder – 1 tbsp, 1 tsp, 1 tbsp
  • Cumin, fennel seeds – 1 tsp each
  • Black and green cardamom – 2, 3 pods
  • Cloves, cinnamon – 4-5, 1-inch piece
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Coriander, ginger, green chilies, lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add meat and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in ginger and garlic paste.
  4. Add spices and salt, then mix in flour paste to thicken.
  5. Add water and simmer for 4–6 hours.
  6. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish before serving.

Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat, spices, and aromatic basmati rice. It is a staple in South Asian cuisine, known for its rich flavor and colorful presentation.

Ingredients

  • Chicken – 1 kg, cut into pieces
  • Basmati rice – 3 cups
  • Yogurt – 1 cup
  • Onions – 2 large, sliced
  • Tomatoes – 2, chopped
  • Ginger and garlic paste – 1 tbsp each
  • Green chilies – 4, sliced
  • Mint and coriander leaves – ½ cup each
  • Lemon juice – 2 tbsp
  • Red chili, turmeric, coriander powder – 1 tsp each
  • Whole spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves) – 1 tsp mixed
  • Saffron (optional) – soaked in warm milk
  • Oil or ghee – ½ cup
  • Salt – to taste

Steps

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes, then parboil and set aside.
  2. Heat oil and fry onions until golden brown.
  3. Add chicken, ginger, garlic paste, and spices, and cook until chicken is tender.
  4. Add yogurt, tomatoes, and green chilies; simmer until oil separates.
  5. Layer rice and chicken mixture in a pot, adding mint, coriander, and saffron.
  6. Cover and steam on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Mix gently before serving with raita or salad.
