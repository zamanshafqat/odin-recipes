Haleem

Description

Haleem is a hearty, slow-cooked dish popular in South Asia and the Middle East, known for its thick, creamy texture and rich flavor. It is made by cooking meat, wheat, lentils, and barley together with a blend of aromatic spices. The ingredients are simmered for several hours, then blended into a smooth, porridge-like consistency. Haleem is often garnished with fried onions, fresh coriander, lemon wedges, green chilies, and a sprinkle of chaat masala, making it a flavorful and filling meal, traditionally served with naan or paratha.

Ingredients

  • Meat (beef, chicken, or mutton) – 1 kg
  • Wheat, barley, and lentils – 1 cup mixed
  • Onions – 2 large, sliced
  • Ginger and garlic paste – 1 tbsp each
  • Oil or ghee – ½ cup
  • Red chili, turmeric, coriander powder – 1 tbsp, 1 tsp, 1 tbsp
  • Cumin seeds – 1 tsp
  • Black and green cardamom – 2, 3 pods
  • Cloves and cinnamon – 4-5, 1-inch piece
  • Salt – to taste
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Fried onions, coriander, green chilies, lemon wedges – for garnish

Steps

  1. Soak wheat, barley, and lentils for a few hours.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and fry onions until golden brown.
  3. Add meat and cook until browned, then stir in ginger and garlic paste.
  4. Add spices (red chili, turmeric, coriander, cumin, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) and salt.
  5. Add soaked grains and lentils, then pour in water and simmer for 4–6 hours.
  6. Blend the mixture until smooth or use a wooden spoon to mash it.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fried onions, coriander, green chilies, and lemon wedges.
  8. Serve hot with naan or paratha.
