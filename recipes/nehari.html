Nehari

Description

Nehari is a rich, aromatic Pakistani dish known for its deep flavors and slow-cooked tenderness. To prepare nehari, start by heating oil or ghee in a large pot and sautéing sliced onions until golden brown. Add meat—traditionally beef shank, lamb, or mutton—and cook until browned. Stir in a mixture of ginger, garlic paste, and a blend of spices including fennel seeds, cloves, nutmeg, black cardamom, and red chili powder for a robust flavor profile. Add flour dissolved in water to thicken the gravy. Slowly simmer the dish for several hours to tenderize the meat and allow the flavors to meld. Finish with a sprinkle of garam masala, fresh coriander, green chilies, and julienned ginger. Nehari is best enjoyed hot, accompanied by naan or paratha, with lemon wedges on the side to enhance its rich, spicy taste.

Ingredients

  • Meat (beef shank, lamb, or mutton) – 1 kg
  • Onions – 2 large, sliced
  • Ghee or oil – ½ cup
  • Ginger and garlic paste – 1 tbsp each
  • Flour (for thickening) – 2 tbsp
  • Water – 6 to 8 cups
  • Red chili, turmeric, coriander powder – 1 tbsp, 1 tsp, 1 tbsp
  • Cumin, fennel seeds – 1 tsp each
  • Black and green cardamom – 2, 3 pods
  • Cloves, cinnamon – 4-5, 1-inch piece
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Coriander, ginger, green chilies, lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and sauté sliced onions until golden brown.
  2. Add meat and cook until browned
  3. Stir in ginger and garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add spices (red chili, turmeric, coriander, cumin, fennel, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, nutmeg, mace) and salt.
  5. Mix flour with water to make a smooth paste, then stir it into the pot to thicken the gravy.
  6. Pour in 6–8 cups of water and simmer on low heat for 4–6 hours until the meat is tender.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala before removing from heat.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander, ginger, green chilies, and serve with lemon wedges.
